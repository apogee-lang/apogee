# Draft Email to Incorporation Attorney

**Subject:** Formation of Apogee Language Foundation, Inc. — Delaware Nonprofit LLC

---

Dear [Attorney Name],

I'm writing to engage your firm for the formation of a Delaware nonprofit entity for an open source programming language project. Below is a summary of the structure we need.

## Entity

**Name:** Apogee Language Foundation, Inc.
**Type:** Delaware Limited Liability Company, organized as a nonprofit trade association
**Tax Status:** 501(c)(6) — business league / trade association (not a 501(c)(3) charity)

We chose 501(c)(6) because the Foundation's primary purpose is to advance the interests of a professional community (software developers using the Apogee language), funded by corporate membership dues. This mirrors the structure used by the Python Software Foundation (which is a 501(c)(3) but considered switching to (c)(6)), the Rust Foundation, and the Linux Foundation.

## Purpose

The Foundation will:
1. Own and maintain the Apogee programming language specification
2. Maintain the reference implementation (open source compiler)
3. Manage the trademark "Apogee" for the programming language
4. Accept corporate membership dues and donations
5. Fund development, infrastructure, and community activities

## Intellectual Property

- The language **specification** will be owned by the Foundation, licensed under CC BY 4.0
- All **source code** is licensed under Apache License 2.0
- The **trademark** "Apogee" (for programming language use) will be owned by the Foundation
- A royalty-free **patent policy** applies to all contributors
- Contributors sign a standard CLA (Contributor License Agreement) granting Apache 2.0 rights

We need your guidance on:
- Whether a CLA is necessary given Apache 2.0 licensing, or if a DCO (Developer Certificate of Origin) suffices
- Trademark registration strategy (USPTO classes for software/programming languages)
- IP assignment mechanics from the founding contributor (myself) to the Foundation

## Governance

- **Board of Directors:** 5 members (3 community-elected, 1 corporate-elected, 1 board-appointed)
- **Terms:** 2 years, staggered, 3-term limit
- **Officers:** President, Treasurer, Secretary (elected by the Board from its members)
- **Corporate Membership:** 3 tiers ($5K, $25K, $100K annually) — advisory and roadmap input, not design control
- **Conflict of interest policy:** annual disclosure, recusal from conflicted votes

Draft governance documents are available at: https://github.com/apogee-lang/apogee/blob/main/GOVERNANCE.md

## Funding Model

Initial funding will come from:
1. Corporate membership dues (targeting $500K Year 1)
2. Individual donations (via GitHub Sponsors and Open Collective)
3. Potentially grants from technology foundations

We need guidance on:
- 501(c)(6) application process and timeline
- Whether membership dues are tax-deductible for corporate members (as business expenses)
- Annual reporting requirements
- State tax obligations (Delaware and any nexus states)

## Timeline

We would like to:
1. File the Delaware LLC formation as soon as possible
2. Apply for EIN immediately after formation
3. Begin 501(c)(6) application within 30 days
4. Have the entity operational (able to accept membership dues) within 60 days
