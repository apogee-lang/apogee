## Article I — Name and Purpose

### 1.1 Name

The name of the organization is **Apogee Language Foundation, Inc.** (hereinafter "the Foundation").

### 1.2 Purpose

The Foundation is organized exclusively for the following purposes:

1. To develop, maintain, and advance the Apogee programming language specification
2. To maintain and distribute the reference implementation of the Apogee compiler
3. To foster an open source community around the Apogee ecosystem
4. To promote programming language research and education in service of safer, more reliable software
5. To manage intellectual property related to the Apogee programming language on behalf of the community

The Foundation operates as a nonprofit trade association under Section 501(c)(6) of the Internal Revenue Code.

## Article II — Intellectual Property

### 2.1 Specification Ownership

The Apogee Language Specification (as published in `spec/SPEC.md` and successor documents) is owned by the Foundation. The specification is licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC BY 4.0), permitting anyone to implement the specification in any compiler.

### 2.2 Code Licensing

All source code in the Apogee reference implementation, standard library, tooling, and associated projects is licensed under the Apache License 2.0. Contributors grant the Foundation a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive license to their contributions.

### 2.3 Trademark

The name "Apogee" and the Apogee logo are trademarks of the Foundation. Use guidelines are published separately. Community projects may use the name "Apogee" in descriptive contexts (e.g., "Apogee plugin for X") without a license, but may not imply endorsement.

### 2.4 Patent Policy

The Foundation adopts a royalty-free patent policy. All contributors to the specification or reference implementation agree that any patents they hold covering the contributed material are licensed royalty-free to all implementors of the specification.

## Article III — Membership

### 3.1 Membership Tiers

| Tier | Annual Dues | Governance Rights | Benefits |
|------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Individual** | $0 | Vote in Core Team elections (if active contributor) | Community access, contributor recognition |
| **Associate** | $5,000/year | 1 advisory vote on roadmap priorities | Logo on website (small), roadmap input, early access to releases |
| **Supporting** | $25,000/year | 1 binding vote on roadmap priorities | Logo on website (medium), priority bug triage, quarterly briefings |
| **Platinum** | $100,000/year | 1 Board seat (observer), 2 binding votes on roadmap priorities | Logo on website (large), dedicated support channel, annual on-site review |

### 3.2 Founding Member Discount

The first 10 corporate members at any tier receive a 50% discount on their first year's dues.

### 3.3 Membership Term

Corporate memberships are annual, renewable. Membership dues are non-refundable. Members may withdraw at any time but are not entitled to a prorated refund.

### 3.4 Membership Does Not Confer Control

Corporate members advise on roadmap priorities but do not control language design. Language design decisions follow the AEP process described in GOVERNANCE.md and are made by the Core Team.

## Article IV — Board of Directors

### 4.1 Composition

The Board consists of:

- 3 seats elected by Individual members (active contributors)
- 1 seat elected by Corporate members (Supporting and Platinum tiers)
- 1 seat appointed by the Board (independent technical expert)

Total: 5 Board members.

### 4.2 Terms

Board members serve 2-year terms, staggered so that no more than 3 seats are up for election in any year. There is a limit of 3 consecutive terms (6 years).

### 4.3 Officers

The Board elects from its members:

- **President** — External representation, signing authority
- **Treasurer** — Financial oversight, budget approval
- **Secretary** — Meeting minutes, governance compliance

### 4.4 Meetings

The Board meets quarterly (minimum). Meetings may be conducted remotely. A quorum is 3 of 5 members. Decisions require simple majority unless otherwise specified.

### 4.5 Compensation

Board members serve without compensation. Reasonable travel expenses for Foundation business are reimbursed.

---

## Article V — Conflict of Interest

### 5.1 Disclosure

Board members and Core Team members must disclose any financial interest in decisions before the Foundation. This includes employment by a corporate member, equity in a company that would benefit from a decision, or consulting relationships.

### 5.2 Recusal

A member with a disclosed conflict of interest must recuse themselves from voting on the conflicted matter. They may participate in discussion but not in the decision.

### 5.3 Annual Disclosure

All Board and Core Team members submit an annual conflict of interest disclosure listing their employer, board seats, and significant financial interests relevant to the Foundation's work.

---

## Article VI — Finances

### 6.1 Funding Sources

- Corporate membership dues
- Individual donations
- Grants from technology foundations
- Conference and event revenue
- Merchandise

### 6.2 Budget

The Treasurer presents an annual budget for Board approval. The budget allocates funds to:

- Infrastructure (CI/CD, hosting, domains)
- Development (contractor or employee compensation for compiler work)
- Community (events, documentation, outreach)
- Legal (trademark, incorporation, compliance)
- Reserve (minimum 6 months operating expenses)

### 6.3 Transparency

Annual financial summaries are published publicly. Detailed financials are available to corporate members.

---

## Article VII — Dissolution

If the Foundation is dissolved:

1. All debts and obligations are satisfied
2. All intellectual property (spec, trademarks) transfers to the Apache Software Foundation or a similar 501(c)(3)/(c)(6) organization, as determined by the Board
3. All source code remains under Apache License 2.0 in perpetuity
4. Remaining assets are distributed to one or more 501(c)(3) organizations with compatible missions

---

## Article VIII — Amendments

These articles may be amended by a 4/5 Board vote with a 30-day public comment period. Article II (Intellectual Property) and Article VII (Dissolution) require unanimous Board approval to amend.
